Non-textbook problems (NTB):

  1. Suppose the following are the percentage of US adults with the following conditions:

    • \(A\): Hypertension 33%

    • \(B\): Diabetes 9%

    • \(C\): Metabolic syndrome 24%

    • \(A\) or \(B\): 39%

    • \(A\) or \(C\): 45%

    • \(B\) or \(C\): 28%

    • \(A\) or \(B\) or \(C\): 48%

    1. Make a Venn diagram of the 3 conditions labeling the percentage (or probability) for ALL of the 8 “sections”. Hint: Start from the last condition and work your way up!
    2. For each of the following (1. - 7. below), (\(i\)) write out the event using unions, intersections, and/or complements of the events \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\) (this is NOT finding the probability, that’s in \(ii\)); (\(ii\)) find the probability of the event; and (\(iii\)) write a sentence explaining what the probability is of in terms of the context of the problem.

      1. \(\mathbb{P}\)(event at least one of the 3)

      2. \(\mathbb{P}\)(event none)

      3. \(\mathbb{P}\)(event \(A\) only)

      4. \(\mathbb{P}\)(event exactly one)

      5. \(\mathbb{P}\)(event \(A\) and \(B\))

      6. \(\mathbb{P}\)(event \(A\) and \(B\) but not \(C\))

      7. \(\mathbb{P}\)(event all 3)

  2. The German word for probability theory is \[W\ A\ H\ R\ S\ C\ H\ E\ I\ N\ L\ I\ C\ H\ K\ E\ I\ T\ S\ T\ H\ E\ O\ R\ I\ E\] If the letters in this word are arranged at random,

    1. what is the probability that none of the H’s will be adjacent?
    2. what is the probability that not all of the H’s will be adjacent?
